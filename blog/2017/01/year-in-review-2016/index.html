ManageIQ Year In Review 2016

Jan 11, 2017 by cybette
  • collaboration

Last year has been pretty exciting and eventful for ManageIQ! If you’re new to the community, or if you just want a refresher, let me be your guide and summarize the cool progress we made throughout 2016.

Development

  • Darga GA release on Jun 7
  • Euwe GA release on Dec 20
  • New blog series Last Week in ManageIQ providing a closer look at some of the pull requests week-to-week
  • Regular Sprint reviews highlighting the efforts and achievements sprint-to-sprint
  • Started work on the Fine release

Events

  • Second ManageIQ Design Summit on June 6-7
  • ManageIQ booth in Community Central at Red Hat Summit on June 27-30
  • Presented at a number of meetups and conferences, listed on the community page
  • Planning for ManageIQ participation at upcoming DevConf.CZ 2017 and FOSDEM 2017
  • Next ManageIQ Design Summit will be around Sept/Oct 2017. Keep an eye on the community page or events posts for updates!

Top content

  • Most read blog posts: Darga GA, Presentation slides from Design Summit, Euwe GA
  • Most viewed slides: Sprint 38, Sprint 43, Sprint 44
  • Most played videos: Self-Service UI Demo, Sprint 38, Sprint 46
  • Forum post with the most replies: Voting on Name of F Release
  • Forum post with the most views: Cloud-init on VMware provider

While it’s interesting to see which content have been popular over the past year, do check out and follow us on SlideShare/YouTube etc. for updates and discover hidden gems, and join the discussions on the Talk forum!

Website and more

  • Redesigned website, a fruitful collaboration between engineering, design, marketing and IT
  • Open Source License Change from dual-license GPL and Apache to single Apache license
  • Contributed article to LWN.net building awareness for the project
  • Consistent increase in ManageIQ site traffic and engagement metrics, > 60% increase in github and forum activities, > 30% increase in social channels from a year ago

Thanks for all your participation and contributions in 2016. There’s more to look forward to in 2017. Stay tuned!
